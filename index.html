Cosmos — Peer Learning Platform

Founder & Developer · Aug 2024 – Present

01

The Problem

💡

"Effort without feedback is noise."

Every year, thousands of CS students in India grind 0-10 hours daily—solving DSA problems, building projects, preparing for interviews. Most don't get the internship.

❓

The core issue: Students don't know which hours actually moved the needle, whether their "progress" is real or performative, or how their preparation compares to peers who succeeded.

Traditional solutions fail: mentors don't scale, communities become noise, accountability apps track tasks—not outcomes.

02

The Insight

🧠

Evidence-Based Design from Learning Science

I spent weeks reviewing 15+ peer-reviewed papers on learning science. The evidence pointed to specific mechanisms:

d = 0.70
Retrieval Practice

Daily proof submissions, not just check-ins

d = 0.65
Implementation Intentions

Goal-setting tied to specific actions

d = 0.40
Cooperative Learning

Pod-based peer feedback loops

✓
Public Commitment

Weekly reflections shared with pod

Most "accountability" apps ignore this research. They gamify streaks without understanding why streaks sometimes work—and when they backfire.

03

The Solution

🛠

Pods: Micro-communities with shared goals

Cosmos organizes students into pods—micro-communities of 5-8 people with similar goals. Each pod runs on a weekly rhythm:

📝
Daily Proof Submissions

Not "I studied 4 hours" but "Here's the problem I solved, here's where I got stuck"

👥
Peer Feedback

Pod members review each other's work, catch blind spots

🔥
Streak Tracking

Consistency signals, not vanity metrics

💬
Weekly Reflections

What worked? What didn't? Shared transparently

React + Vite Firebase Real-time Sync Auth System

Honest Challenges

Empty pods kill retention: student joins, sees no activity, leaves. Death spiral.

What I'm iterating on: Consolidating to 1-3 genuinely active pods before scaling, community-led growth via existing WhatsApp/Telegram groups, activation mechanics.

"This is the hardest part of building a community product. No amount of features fixes an empty room."

🔮 What's Next

AI-powered journal analysis: Integrating Gemini API to surface patterns—"You've spent 60% of time on easy problems" or "Your stuck points cluster around dynamic programming."

The goal: make effort legible.
